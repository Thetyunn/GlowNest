Oily Skin

Oily skin is a skin type where the sebaceous glands produce too much sebum (natural oil). This leads to a shiny appearance, especially on the forehead, nose, and chin (the T-zone), along with visibly enlarged pores and a higher likelihood of blackheads and acne breakouts.

How to care
Dry Skin

Dry skin is a skin type that lacks sufficient moisture and natural oils (sebum). It often feels tight, rough, or flaky, and may look dull or show fine lines more easily. It can be caused by genetics, weather (like cold or dry air), harsh soaps, hot water, or aging.The key is to hydrate to skin.

How to care
Sensitive Skin

Sensitive skin is a reactive skin condition that gets easily irritated by ingredients, products, weather, or stress. It may feel itchy, sting, burn, turn red, or develop rashes after contact with triggers. It needs gentle, fragrance-free, hypoallergenic products and consistent, soothing care.

How to care
Combination Skin

Combination skin is a common skin type where the T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin) is oily with enlarged pores, while the cheeks and eye area are normal to dry. It requires a balanced skincare approach‚Äîaddressing oiliness in the T-zone while providing hydration to the drier areas.
